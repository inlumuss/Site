The Project

Why In Lumuss?

There exists a vast, largely unexplored library of human thought — stretching from Alexandrian papyri to Renaissance alchemical manuscripts, from Sufi mystical poetry to Enlightenment-era star charts. In Lumuss was born from a conviction that this knowledge deserves to live again.

Every month, we dive deep into a single theme — Hermeticism, sacred geometry, forgotten mythologies, the history of astronomy — and distil it into something tangible and beautiful that arrives at your door.

Each envelope holds an art print of museum quality, designed by hand, doubling as wall art or a treasured gift. A handwritten-style letter with curated bibliography. And original stickers drawn from the same symbolic universe.

This is not a subscription to content. It is an invitation to a way of seeing.

The Curator

About Me

I have spent years wandering the corridors of great libraries — the Bodleian, the Biblioteca Nazionale, the dusty shelves of second-hand bookshops in cities I barely remember — drawn by a recurring feeling that there is something worth recovering in the old texts.

"The beginning of wisdom is the discovery of our own ignorance."
— attributed to William Durant

In Lumuss grew from a desire to share that feeling — to make esoteric and occult history approachable, beautiful, and tangible. I design each print by hand, research each theme with care, and write each letter as if writing to a close friend.

The name comes from old Latin: in lux, into the light. Everything here is an attempt to illuminate a corner of the world that has been allowed to grow dark.
